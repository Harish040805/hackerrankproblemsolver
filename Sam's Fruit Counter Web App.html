Sam's Fruit Counter (By Harish)

Instructions

What is this web app about?

This app calculates how many apples and oranges fall on Sam's house based on the positions of the house and trees and the distances fruits fall.

Practical Use of This Concept:

This problem demonstrates a simple example of coordinate mapping and range checking in programming, which is widely used in:

  • Game development: Detecting whether an object (like a character or projectile) lands in a specific area or hits a target.
  • Simulations: Modeling real-world phenomena, such as rainfall landing on a plot, or traffic simulations where vehicles enter a zone.
  • Physics/Engineering calculations: Determining if moving particles, objects, or forces intersect specific regions.
  • Data visualization: Mapping numeric values to positions on a chart or number line, just like our fruits landing visually on the number line.

How to use:

  • Enter the start (s) and end (t) positions of Sam's house on a number line.
  • Enter the position of the apple tree (a) and orange tree (b).
  • Enter the distances each apple and orange falls from its tree as comma-separated numbers.
  • Click "Update Number Line" to see the result visually.

Input guidelines:

  • House positions (s, t) and tree positions (a, b) must be integers.
  • Fruit distances can be positive (fall to the right) or negative (fall to the left).
  • Apple and orange distances should be entered as comma-separated integers (Advisable to give).

Output:

  • The result shows two numbers:
  • Apples on house: Number of apples landing on the house.
  • Oranges on house: Number of oranges landing on the house.
  • Output is inclusive of the house range [s, t].

Example:

      House Start (s): 7
      House End (t): 11 
      Apple Tree Position (a): 5
      Orange Tree Position (b): 15
      Apple Distances (comma separated): -2,2,1
      Orange Distances (comma separated): 5,-6

Result: Apples on house = 1, Oranges on house = 1
